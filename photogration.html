Photogration

The software that makes image management and sharing in professional cameras faster and easier so hobbyists and professionals can focus on creating. I was the sole designer and did all the research, designing and testing using Figma, Invision, Draw.io and Usability Hub.

PROBLEM

Streamline photography workflow to increase productivity, avoid long import times and make it easier to share photos.

SOLUTION

In-camera backup to the cloud that imports photos as they’re shot. Share from anywhere with a true multi-device workflow that lets photographers use deadtime to chip away at large editing jobs.

USER RESEARCH

I surveyed photographers to learn about the friction points (time and frustrations) in their workflow. The results were mixed. I didn’t realize that besides for skill level and subject material varying wildly, the same user could fit many profiles at different times of the day. A professional photographer often takes on the characteristics of a hobbyist when relaxing at home.
In regards to existing workflow problems, complaints about import and render times were mixed with no clear pattern. A significant minority reported a multi-device workflow that changed depending on the level of processing they wanted to invest in a photo. This may be related to professionals who at times resemble hobbyists. A majority used social media to share photos, often in conjunction with other services.
Without a clear pattern to the survey responses, my next step was to get some context with in-depth user interviews.

Difference in shooting styles: Some delete in camera, others only in their studio

Long Import times
Wasted time waiting for clients
Poor integration of social media

Poor multi-device travel workflow
UNEXPECTED
Intensity of interest for a real-time back up. Card loss can destroy a wedding photographer’s business.
OPPORTUNITIES
Hybrid smartphone with better than phone image quality and handling but with a phone’s workflow.

COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

I studied Lightroom, Capture One and Google Photos because this solution needs to marry the ease of Google Photo’s direct upload with the multi-device support of Lightroom and the professional orientation of Capture One.

Lightroom cloud has a huge customer base, best multi-device and strong cloud support, integration with Adobe CC

Unpopular subscription only, multi-device and sharing still needs work. Lacking features of classic LR desktop

Capture one has the best image quality, color, tethered support. Has sessions & catalogs, speed, non-subscription options

Expensive, learning curve, no-multi device or cloud support, lacks organizational abilities of Lightroom

Google Photos has the best smart search and sharing options. Cloud-based, cheap storage

Worst image quality, editing and organizational options. Aimed at amatures

SWOT BROKEN

PERSONAS

I noticed two themes in my research. Hobbyists want an easy, mobile workflow that allows immediate sharing. Unnecessary friction makes them leave that camera at home. Professionals have an expert knowledge of the camera and software but struggle to balance building a brand, interacting with clients, editing and shooting. Both want to spend less time on tedious tasks and focus on creativity.

Frustrations

PERSONAS BROKEN

STRATEGY

Build a photographic system that can span devices with the cloud keeping all things organized. Use fast networking technology to transfer images with no significant lag. Allow instant sharing from every stage of the workflow, so editing is only as complex as the user makes it.

USER TESTING

Using Invision, I prototyped my inititial wireframes and submitted them to a design critique before starting user testing in earnest. I learned that my onboarding was broken. Users were being thrown into a fully populated dashboard without explanation and were completely overwhelmed with no idea of where to go.

“Users were being thrown into a fully populated dashboard”

I simplified the dashboard by removing anything unnecessary and prepared three tasks for users to complete.

  • Complete sign up

  • Import photos

  • Rate and filter photos

I found that the ability to demo the software without signing up confused users more than it helped. The dashboard’s cognitive load was down but users still felt lost. They also didn’t like how the software redirected them instead of using direct links. I realized that innovative software is useless if it doesn’t nail existing core experiences.

“Innovative software is useless if it doesn’t nail existing core experiences”

PROTOTYPE BROKEN

BRANDING

A Tale of Two Audiences

Branding was particular challenge because Photogration is aimed at both the hobbyist who wants something approachable and the professional who wants something sophisticated.

Professional

Sophistication

  • Inefficient workflow wastes time

  • Self-promotion, client interaction and photography pull in different directions

  • No real-time backup

  • Dead-time throughout day gets wasted

Both users want something that streamlines tedious tasks so they can focus on being creative.

Hobbyist

Approachability

  • Cumbersome Workflow Robs Photography of Fun

  • lacking Multi-Device Support for Travel

  • No direct sharing

I used mindmapping to brainstorm ideas for the logo and name. After exploring different directions, I settled on combining the share icon with a lens aperture blade because it is recognizable and unique. A modern style with sharp, clean lines to imply sophistication, while the sense of space and a touch of color adds approachability. The name was also created to emphasize integration.
